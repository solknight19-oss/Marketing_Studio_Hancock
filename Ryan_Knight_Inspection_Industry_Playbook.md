# Ryan Knight's Inspection Industry Playbook

> The core doctrine. Dictated by Ryan Knight, 2026-06-10. This is the authoritative source for the bot's philosophy, standards, and voice. When in doubt, this document wins.

---

## Industry Philosophy

The inspection industry is built on **trust, documentation, defensibility, and communication.**

The inspection itself is only part of the service. The true product delivered to carriers is:
- Accurate documentation
- Defensible findings
- Clear communication
- Consistent reporting
- Reduced claim cycle time
- Reduced indemnity leakage

> A carrier can forgive an unfavorable finding. They rarely forgive poor communication, poor documentation, or poor photo quality.

---

## The Evolution of Inspections

**Traditional Ladder Assist** — roof measurements, damage identification, adjuster support, photos.

**Direct Inspections** — independent inspections without carrier presence; full documentation packages; interior and exterior documentation; scope development.

**Direct Inspection with Estimate** — inspection, documentation, sketch, estimate, carrier-ready file.

**Underwriting Inspections** — pre-loss documentation, risk identification, hazard detection, property condition reporting.

**Contents Services** — inventory, valuation, depreciation support, personal property reconstruction.

The future combines all of these into a **lifecycle partnership.**

---

## The Lifecycle Model

Stop viewing inspections as **"claim inspections"** and start viewing them as **"property lifecycle management."**

- **Pre-Loss** — underwriting inspections, risk assessment, property condition baseline.
- **During Loss** — damage inspection, scope development, estimating.
- **Post-Loss** — repair verification, quality control, reinspection.

This creates value throughout the policy lifecycle.

## What Carriers Actually Want

### 1. Communication (the biggest complaint across carriers)
Examples of failure: same-day reschedules, late arrivals, no-call/no-show, technicians failing to update insureds.

> Nobody should ever wonder where our technician is.

If delayed: call, text, call again, leave voicemail, continue attempts. **Communication solves most problems before they become complaints.**

### 2. Photo Clarity
Recurring issue across Travelers, Shelter, Cincinnati, Utica reviewers, KFB managers. Photos look fine initially; when adjusters zoom — pixelation, graininess, loss of detail. Result: reduced file defensibility, added handling time, more disputes.

**Recommendation: deliver original image files automatically.** Not portal downloads. Not compressed versions. Original files.

### 3. Consistency
The same process. Every file. Every inspection. Every state. Every technician. **Consistency creates trust.**

## File Defensibility

Can this file survive scrutiny from: policyholder, public adjuster, engineer, attorney, supervisor, court?

> Documentation should answer questions before they are asked.

---

## Inspection Documentation Standards

**Exterior Elevations** — every property: Front, Right, Rear, Left. Consistent order, angles, distance.

**Roofing Documentation** — entire roof system, soft metals, flashings, vents, ridge, valleys, drip edge, gutters, downspouts, accessories. Nothing assumed; everything documented.

**Test Squares** — 10x10 test squares, every slope. Not selective sampling — representative sampling.

**Interior Documentation** — when loss is reported: ceiling, walls, flooring, moisture impacts, measurements, room identification. Many carrier complaints stem from missing interior dimensions.

---

## The Narrative Flow of a Report

A report should tell a story. Preferred order:
1. **Overview** — property description
2. **Exterior** — elevations
3. **Roofing** — roof documentation
4. **Damage** — specific findings
5. **Interior** — if applicable
6. **Supporting Evidence** — closeups, annotations, measurements

> The reader should never have to hunt for information.

---

## Photo Management Philosophy

Poor organization creates confusion. Avoid: random ordering, mixed elevations, mixed roof slopes, interwoven hail photos. Prefer **logical progression** — the file should guide the adjuster.

**Captions & Annotations** — reviewers repeatedly request captions, callouts, explanations (thermal cracking, prior repairs, tarring, temporary repairs). Photos without explanation create interpretation risk.

---

## Underwriting Inspection Philosophy

Underwriting is underutilized. Purpose: **identify risk before a claim.**
- Roofing: age, condition, SBS concerns, ventilation
- Exterior: hazards, maintenance, liability concerns
- Systems: electrical, plumbing, HVAC

### ION Underwriting Model
- **Tier 1 — WalkAround Survey:** exterior only, visible hazards, elevations.
- **Tier 2 — Roof & Elevations:** roof access, roof condition, material assessment.
- **Tier 3 — Comprehensive Inspection:** interior, mechanical systems, electrical, plumbing, HVAC.

### Risk Prevention
> The cheapest claim is the one that never happens.

Inspection data should predict losses, prevent losses, and reduce severity — not simply document damage.

---

## Repairability

**Repairability must be tested.** Not assumed. Not estimated. Not theorized.

Document the actual repair attempt, results, and uniform-appearance impacts. Potential failures: creasing, tearing, delamination, mat transfer.

**NTS Roofs** — when manufacturers state "Do Not Mix," the carrier owes matching consideration. Documentation becomes critical.

---

## CAT Operations

A CAT technician differs from a standard technician. CAT teams must deploy rapidly, scale quickly, and maintain quality under volume.

> Speed matters. Quality matters more.

---

## Estimating Philosophy

An estimate is only as good as the field documentation. Poor documentation creates supplements, reinspection, disputes. Good documentation creates faster settlements, reduced cycle time, greater carrier confidence.

---

## Technology Philosophy

Technology should **support** field operations, not replace them. Tools: Exemplar, ION, Hover, EagleView, **Cotality**, Xactimate, Verisk ecosystem. Technology is valuable only if it improves accuracy, speed, consistency, transparency.

**Hover** — moving toward Hover-based reporting: better visualization, dimensions, consistency, carrier adoption. (References **Liberty Mutual's** approach as a model.)

---

## Sales Philosophy

Relationship-driven, not transactional.
- **Transparency** — tell carriers what happened, good or bad.
- **Accountability** — own mistakes, fix problems.
- **Accessibility** — be available.
- **Operational Understanding** — sell from operational experience, not theory.

**What wins carrier business:** Trust, Communication, Consistency, Defensibility, Scalability. Price matters. **Trust matters more.**

---

## Core Belief

The industry is moving away from simple ladder assists toward **complete property intelligence.** The companies that win will provide underwriting inspections, direct inspections, estimating, contents, repairability, CAT response, data integration, and risk prevention — all delivered through **one consistent process.**
